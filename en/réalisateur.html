THE DIRECTOR

Louis Leterrier

Louis Leterrier is a French director and producer, born on 17 June 1973 in Paris. He is best known for his French blockbusters (The Transporter, Danny the Dog, The Transporter 2) and American blockbusters (The Incredible Hulk, Clash of the Titans, Elusive, Fast and Furious 10). He is one of the few French directors to have directed productions with budgets in excess of 100 million dollars. He is the only French director to have earned more than $2 billion from his films.

Louis Leterrier is the son of director François Leterrier and artistic director Catherine Leterrier. He began his career as an assistant director on films such as Alien: The Resurrection, Joan of Arc and Asterix and Obelix: Mission Cleopatra. He moved into directing in 2002 with The Transporter, an action film co-directed with Corey Yuen and produced by Luc Besson. He then entered the "Besson stable", along with Chris Nahon and Pierre Morel.

He came to prominence in 2004 with Danny the Dog, a drama starring Jet Li as a man who is treated like a dog. He then directed The Transporter 2 in 2005, which confirmed its commercial success. In 2008, he moved to Hollywood to direct The Incredible Hulk, starring Edward Norton as the green superhero. This was his first big-budget American film.

In 2010, he directed Clash of the Titans, a remake of the 1981 film of the same name, which revisits Greek mythology with Sam Worthington in the role of Perseus. The film was a box-office hit worldwide, despite mixed reviews. In 2013, he returned with Elusive, a heist film mixing magic and illusion, starring Jesse Eisenberg, Mark Ruffalo, Woody Harrelson and Mélanie Laurent. The film was a surprise hit, leading to a sequel in 2016.

In 2019, he will direct Dark Crystal: Time of the Resistance, a prequel series to Jim Henson and Frank Oz's cult 1982 film. The series uses animatronic puppets and digital special effects to create a fantasy world populated by strange creatures. The series received rave reviews and won an Emmy Award.

In 2021, he directed Lupin, dans l'ombre d'Arsène, a French series broadcast on Netflix, based on the character of Arsène Lupin created by Maurice Leblanc. The series stars Omar Sy as Assane Diop, a gentleman burglar who seeks to avenge his father, who has been unjustly accused of theft. The series was an international success and became the most watched series on Netflix in January 2021.
